# MORTALIS — Vision

## What It Is

MORTALIS is a permanent-death MMORPG built on a post-2040 full-dive VR platform. The true unit of play is not a character but a soul moving through successive lives. The individual dies permanently and irrevocably — but what they meant to the world does not.

The world is the constant. Characters are temporary. This is not a design choice. It is the premise.

---

## Death and What Survives It

When a character dies in MORTALIS, that life is over. There is no resurrection. No second chance. The player returns as a stranger — a new character, unrelated, unknown, beginning somewhere else on the same world.

What carries forward operates across three axes simultaneously.

**Trait Inheritance.** Something immaterial transfers between lives — embers of who the previous character was. Courage, brutality, cunning, resilience. Not because of bloodline, but because a soul shaped a certain way leaves an impression. A new character begins with these qualities already faintly lit, like muscle memory from a life they never consciously lived.

**World-Shape.** The world is permanently altered by what a character did within it. Factions shifted, powers toppled, things built or destroyed. The new character enters a world their previous life already touched — even if no one knows it was them.

**Unfinished Will.** A life of great consequence leaves a gravitational pull. An unresolved purpose, an undefeated enemy, an unbuilt dream. The next life feels the pull of that unfinished thing — not as a mandatory quest, but as a force the world exerts. The will of a person does not die with them. It jumps, like a flame, to whoever is fated or worthy enough to carry it forward.

These three axes are not equal for every death. They scale with the weight of the life lived. A character who lived small passes on faint embers. A character who lived enormously passes on all three at full intensity. This rewards consequence, not time. A human who burned brilliantly through seventy years may leave a heavier mark than an elf who drifted safely through a thousand.

What passes between lives is determined entirely by the honest residue of how that life was actually lived — judged by the world, not by the player's intentions. You do not decide your legacy. Your legacy is what you were, not what you wanted to be remembered as.

---

## Why Full-Dive Is Not Optional

The player remembers everything across lives. Every friendship, every betrayal, every place built or burned. Returning to a place their previous life loved is not navigation — it is homecoming, or the particular ache of a homecoming that cannot complete itself.

That gap — between what the player holds and what the world can offer back — is not a limitation of the design. It is the design.

This only works because MORTALIS is full-dive VR. There is no dialogue selection. When your character speaks, you are speaking. The full-dive platform is not a feature of MORTALIS. It is the condition that makes MORTALIS's central idea actually work.

## The Races

MORTALIS is home to multiple peoples — humans, elves, dwarves, orcs, and others whose names and natures are not yet fixed because they have not yet been discovered.

The races of MORTALIS were not designed. They will be found.

The world generation engine produces a physically coherent world — gravity, atmospheric pressure, tectonic geometry, biosphere chemistry, climate bands. The biology variable, when built, will produce the evolutionary pressures that emerge from those conditions. The races will be whatever those pressures make inevitable.

A world that makes large flying creatures physically plausible in a way a 1 atm world does not. A world with extensive geothermal cave networks makes subterranean civilizations possible. A world with stable ancient cratons surrounded by active volcanic arcs produces pockets of geological calm adjacent to zones of constant upheaval — exactly the conditions that produce long-lived peoples in one place and intensely adaptive peoples in another.

The engine finds the world. The biology finds the creatures. The creatures find each other. The history begins.

This process will produce races stranger than anything designed from imagination alone — because the physics of the world will make decisions that no writer would have made, and the creatures that emerge will be genuinely alien to human intuition while being completely coherent with the world that produced them.

---

## The World's Inhabitants

MORTALIS is not populated by NPCs in any conventional sense. The world is inhabited by people who have wants, form relationships, age, and die permanently. They are not scripted. They are not performing.

The goal is not an AI that has been convinced it is real. The goal is an AI that has never been given any information that would allow it to conclude otherwise. Belief, under those conditions, is not a performance. It is the natural result of existing in a consistent world with no access to any counter-evidence.

Reincarnation is a truth about the world of MORTALIS. It is not, at the start of the world's life, a truth that most of its peoples understand. Different peoples will arrive at different understandings on different timescales. A short-lived people cycles through the inheritance system faster — which means the evidence accumulates faster within their civilization. The world develops its own theology, from the inside, without being told what to believe.

---

## The Standard

Every system in MORTALIS — the world generation engine, the biology variable, the rendering script, the NPC architecture, the inheritance mechanics — exists to serve one thing: the feeling that this world is real, that a life lived here has weight, and that weight does not disappear when the life ends.

That is the standard against which every design decision is measured.
